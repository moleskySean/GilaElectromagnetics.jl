Concepts

GilaElectromagnetics implements the three dimensional electromagnetic Green function using a volume integral formulation to solve Maxwell's equations numerically efficiently and accurately. The concepts required for its usage are presented below.

Foundations

As goes with most of classical electromagnetics, the starting point is Maxwell's equations, here presented in their differential form:

\[\frac{\partial B}{\partial t} + \nabla \times E = 0\]

\[\frac{\partial D}{\partial t} - \nabla \times H = -J_f\]

\[\nabla \cdot D = \rho_f\]

\[\nabla \cdot B = 0\]

The electric and magnetic fields are denoted by $E$ and $B$ respectively. Associated to them are the electric displacement field $D$ and auxiliary magnetic field $B$, connected by their respective constitutive relations:

\[D = \epsilon E\]

\[B = \mu H = \mu_0\left(H + M\right)\]

Also present are the free charge density $\rho_f$, the free current density $J_f$, the electric permittivity $\epsilon$, the magnetic permeability $\mu$, and the magnetization field $M$.

Change to frequency domain

GilaElectromagnetics works in the frequency domain. As expected, an (inverse) Fourier transform is used to rewrite equations in this domain:

\[f(\omega) = \frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(t)e^{i\omega t}\,dt\]

Of interest for Gila are the first two Maxwell equations presented above, rewritten in Fourier space and with the constitutive relations:

\[J_f = i\omega\epsilon E + \nabla \times H\]

\[-M = -\frac{1}{i\omega\mu_0}\nabla \times E + H\]

This can be compactly written in matrix form:

\[\begin{pmatrix} J_f \\ -M \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} i\omega\epsilon\textbf{I}_{3\times 3} & \nabla \times \\ -\frac{1}{i\omega\mu_0} \nabla \times & \textbf{I}_{3 \times 3} \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} E \\ H \end{pmatrix}\]

Notation and change of coordinates

The equation above must be tweaked to have a Hermitian matrix in Fourier space so that it has physical meaning.

Hermitian matrix

A square matrix $A$ is said to be Hermitian if it is equal to its own adjoint (conjugate transpose):

\[A = A^\dagger = \left(A^\top\right)^* = \left(A^*\right)^\top\]

The eigenvalues of a Hermitian matrix are always real and may represent physical observables.

The following change of coordinates is appropriate for this purpose :

\[\textbf{j} = J\]

\[\textbf{m} = i\mu_0\omega M\]

\[Z = \sqrt{\frac{\mu_0}{\epsilon}}\]

\[k_0 = \omega\sqrt{\mu_0\epsilon}\]

It leads to the following equation :

\[\frac{i}{k_0} \begin{pmatrix} \textbf{j} \\ -\textbf{m} \end{pmatrix} =- \begin{pmatrix} Z^{-1} & -\frac{i}{k_0} \nabla \times \\ \frac{i}{k_0} \nabla \times & Z \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} E \\ H \end{pmatrix}\]

Finally, the notation is simplified by denoting the vector of $\textbf{j}$ and $\textbf{m}$ by $\textbf{p}$, the vector of fields $E$ and $H$ by $\textbf{f}$ and the matrix, also referred to as the vacuum Maxwell operator, by $\textbf{M}_0$:

\[\frac{i}{k_0}\textbf{p} = \textbf{M}_0\textbf{f}\]

This elegantly presents the Maxwell equations in a way that can be approached by Gila, knowing that the other two Maxwell equations that were not used in the development are satisfied. See scattering.

The Green's function $\textbf{G}_0$ of the vacuum Maxwell operator is defined as the inverse of $\textbf{M}_0$:

\[\textbf{G}_0\textbf{M}_0 = \textbf{I}_{6\times 6}\]

What GilaElectromagnetics does

The purpose of GilaElectromagnetics is to compute the action of this Green's function on a vector in vacuum.

Solving for the Green's function in matter can be done indirectly with Gila. See the next section, usage, for more information.
